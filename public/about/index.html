I’m currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Konstanz. Before that, I completed my doctoral studies at Queen Mary University of London (2021-2025), my master’s studies at Indiana University (2018-2021), and my bachelor’s studies at the University of Georgia (2012-2017).

My work sits at the intersection of sociolinguistics, psycholinguistics, and social psychology. In my research, I primarily use experimental methods to better understand the ways in which sociolinguistic variation is represented in the mind, as well as the perceptual and behavioral outcomes of these representations. I draw on a range of innovative, quantitative approaches in my work, including eye tracking, social-cuing paradigms in speech perception, and implicit-stereotype priming. Much of my work focuses on accent perception and phonetic variation, particularly across varieties of French and English.

I’m increasingly interested in ways that linguistics can be used to reveal and help rectify societal inequalities. I’m a firm advocate for open science practices and for making research as accessible and inclusive as possible.
